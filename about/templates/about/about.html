About Us

In August 2024, solicitors Hannah Unger, Demi Mullen, and Carrie McMeel founded Dog Law Ireland (or DLÍ, which is also the Irish word for “Law”).

Our Purpose

We came together at a pivotal point in Ireland’s dog control landscape - shortly after the announcement of Ireland’s plan to have its first banned type of dog. It was at that time that something we’d all long been aware of was highlighted to us once again - very few people actually understand the laws relating to dogs in Ireland.  We believe that education and enforcement are key to improving life for dogs… & it is with this in mind that we formed Dog Law Ireland. Our journey began with a simple yet powerful idea: to educate people, to advocate for better laws and to push for greater enforcement. 

We cannot change what we don’t understand... so we want to make dog law accessible and easily understood by everyone - the general public, animal rescues, An Garda Siochana, the Government, the veterinarian community, the legal profession & all those working in the canine industry. 

While we are small in number, we are determined to make a change. We are also very fortunate to have developed so many wonderful relationships with those who have been at the forefront of advocating for Ireland’s dogs long before Dog Law Ireland became Dog Law Ireland. 

We also have some wonderful people volunteering their time to help us & we are always open to hearing from people who would like to help (in particular legal researchers!) so please do get in touch if you would like to get involved.

Meet the DLI Team

Hannah Unger

6 years qualified, public and regulatory law solicitor at Fieldfisher LLP. Hannah manages a pro bono team in Fieldfisher specifically relating to reviewing dog laws and setting out proposed reforms. In May 2024, Hannah published a paper on “Key Reforms to Dog Breeding” and presented her report in the AV Room of the Oireachtas. Hannah also advises dog rescues and dog shelters in relation to legal issues as they arise for them and Control of Dogs Act 1986. Hannah has also assisted drafting their policies and procedures. Hannah fosters dogs and adopted the gorgeous Twinkle from Dogs Angels in August 2023.

Demi Mullen

6 years qualified, investment funds solicitor. Demi co-established the Animal Law Network Ireland along with a colleague in 2024 - a group for legal professionals and academics with an interest in animal law in Ireland.
Demi has presented on dog law in Ireland at the UK Centre for Animal Law Conference and Causeway Coast Dog Rescue’s Companion Animal Welfare Conference. Demi adopted her gorgeous furbaby Klaus from Cara Rescue in February 2019.

Carrie McMeel

5 years qualified, commercial, data protection and regulatory solicitor. Carrie regularly advises dog rescues in relation to legal issues as they arise for them. She has also extensively advised and drafted policies, agreements and procedures for dog rescues.
Carrie gives countless hours of volunteering at her local rescue, Dogs Aid and is the proud owner of her two gorgeous rescue dogs; Dots (Collon) & Zag (Dogs Aid).
